üìç Esophageal Diverticulum

Zenker Diverticulum: Pulsion Diverticulum in the back (Z is in the back of the alphabet).

The site of weakness is the Killian Dehiscence or triangle. Another point of trivia is that the diverticulum arises from the hypopharynx (not the cervical esophagus).

Killian-Jamieson Pulsion Diverticulum: Anterior and lateral protrusion through an area below the cricopharyngeus muscle and lateral to the ligaments suspending the esophagus on the cricoid cartilage. Located in the cervical esophagus.

Epiphrenic Diverticula: Located just above the diaphragm (usually on the RIGHT). The para-esophageal hernia is usually on the LEFT. They are considered pulsion types (associated with motor abnormality).

  • Round in shape
  • Will NOT empty (contains no muscle in the walls)
